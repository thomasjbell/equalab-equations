## Design Context

### Users
Young professionals, students, and academics (primarily 17–30) solving physics, maths, and chemistry equations. Used during study sessions, problem-sets, and on-the-job calculations. Context: focused, task-oriented — they came to solve something specific, fast.

### Brand Personality
Educational, clean, precise. Three words: **sharp, approachable, exact**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark mode only. No light/dark toggle. The UI lives in focused-work environments — lecture theatres, labs, study sessions at night. A dark, blue-tinted neutral palette with a vivid cyan accent. Typography uses Urbanist — geometric, clean, strong weight contrast for hierarchy. Not a gaming calculator. Not Wolfram Alpha's dense reference-book density. Closer to a well-designed STEM textbook with modern app sensibility.

Anti-references: not glowing-neon cyberpunk, not pastel educational, not generic white SaaS dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. **Hierarchy through weight, not decoration** — let font weight + size do the work; avoid gradient text, badges-on-everything, icon-per-heading patterns
2. **Dark neutrals, one accent** — blue-tinted dark backgrounds, cyan (#0ea5e9-ish) as the single action color
3. **Results are the hero** — equation outputs deserve visual prominence; input fields are secondary
4. **Density without clutter** — many equations per screen, but each card breathes; generous internal padding, tight external spacing
5. **Fast to filter, easy to find** — search is the primary action, category navigation is secondary
